Our Services

Exterior framing

Floor joists are horizontal structural members that span an open space, often between beams, which subsequently transfer the load to vertical structural members. These joists, part of the floor system, carry the weight of everything inside a room, including walls, furniture, appliances, and even people.

Interior framing

Vertical components known as studs and horizontal components known as joists are connected to provide a stable frame for walls, ceilings and floors.

Hanging drywall

Drywall is a construction material used to create walls and ceilings. It's also used to create many design features, including eaves, arches and other architectural specialties. It's quick and easy to install, incredibly durable, and requires only simple repairs when damaged.

Acoustic ceiling

The acoustic ceiling dampens the noise and absorbs a part of the sound through the hole pattern and acoustic fleece. More precisely, it reduces the reverberation in the room. Acoustic ceilings reduce reverberation and prevent it from disturbing conversations.

Door installation

It’s inevitable that some of the air used to heat or cool your home will escape and some of the air from the outside will find its way in, but having a properly-installed front door can limit these heating and cooling inefficiencies. A tightly-sealed and properly-installed new front door can make sure you get the most bang for your buck when it comes to heating and cooling. This is a plus not only for your wallet but for the environment as well.

Exterior Sheathing

It refers to boards or panels that line the walls, floor, and roof. While exterior sheathing is made from several types of materials, the basic function remains the same: create a surface on which other materials can be placed to increase the stability of the home while providing increased insulation.

Rigid insulation

Rigid insulation provides thermal protection and it can also serve as an air and moisture barrier.

Follow the Plan

You need blueprints to estimate the cost of labor and the bill of materials, to create a construction schedule, and to obtain building permits. A set of blueprints must show that your building design is in compliance with your local building codes, or the building inspection department won't approve your permit to begin construction.

Interior Insulation

Insulated interior walls will prevent large temperature swings that can crack drywall. These insulated rooms will also hold their temperature longer. For rooms that aren't used frequently, interior wall insulation can reduce heat transfer and help you save on your utility bill.

Demolition

When you need to create a new room, open space, or wall. You first need to demolish the existing wall if any so that you may apply the changes and create the new vision.
